# LaxHornet Metric Recommendations

## Process Impact Index

- Purpose: Separate controllable behaviors from raw outcomes.
- Formula/scoring logic: (Smart Play + Successful Clear + Supported Ball Carrier tags + Good Decision/Unsuccessful Execution tags + Recovered After Mistake tags) minus repeat unforced turnovers.
- Required inputs: Existing events plus new process tags.
- Sample calculation: 3 Smart Plays + 2 Clears + 1 support tag - 1 unforced turnover = +5 process index.
- Minimum sample size: One full game or 8+ tracked events.
- Suitable age groups: All; explain more simply for U10/U12.
- Limitations: Requires subjective tagging; should not be compared across parents without calibration.
- Possible misleading interpretations: Do not treat this as a coach grade, permanent label, or proof of character.
- Recommended display language: Process plays: helped the team with repeatable decisions beyond the scoreboard.
- Type: derived metric using direct stats plus subjective tags where noted.


## Possession Quality

- Purpose: Show not just whether possession changed, but whether the next play protected it.
- Formula/scoring logic: Extra Possessions Created + retained-follow-up bonus - immediate possession-loss penalty.
- Required inputs: Ground Ball, Faceoff Win, Save, Caused Turnover, Successful Clear, Turnover, Failed Clear, time sequence.
- Sample calculation: GB (+1) followed by clear (+0.5) = +1.5; GB followed by turnover = 0 or -0.5 depending timing.
- Minimum sample size: 3 possession-changing events.
- Suitable age groups: U10+; useful for all positions.
- Limitations: Cannot know team possession after every play unless tracker tags retained/lost.
- Possible misleading interpretations: Do not treat this as a coach grade, permanent label, or proof of character.
- Recommended display language: Possession quality: won chances and turned them into cleaner team possessions.
- Type: derived metric using direct stats plus subjective tags where noted.


## Response After Mistake

- Purpose: Reward constructive recovery after turnovers, failed clears, penalties, or missed chances.
- Formula/scoring logic: Count negative events followed within N events/minutes by hustle, GB, caused turnover, successful clear, smart play, or recovered-after-mistake tag.
- Required inputs: Event sequence and recovery tags.
- Sample calculation: Turnover in Q2 followed by caused turnover within 90 seconds = 1 positive response.
- Minimum sample size: At least one negative event and one later tracked event.
- Suitable age groups: All; especially useful for middle school and older.
- Limitations: A player may respond well without an observable stat.
- Possible misleading interpretations: Do not treat this as a coach grade, permanent label, or proof of character.
- Recommended display language: Response plays: stayed engaged after a tough moment.
- Type: derived metric using direct stats plus subjective tags where noted.


## Team-First Contribution

- Purpose: Capture service behaviors such as support, extra pass, communication, and safe decisions.
- Formula/scoring logic: Assist + Smart Play + Supported Ball Carrier + Communicated Early + Successful Clear + Extra Pass tags.
- Required inputs: Existing assists/clears/smart plays plus subjective tags.
- Sample calculation: 1 assist + 2 support tags + 1 clear = 4 team-first contributions.
- Minimum sample size: One game; stronger over 3 games.
- Suitable age groups: U12+ for nuanced explanation.
- Limitations: Off-ball support is easy to miss from sideline.
- Possible misleading interpretations: Do not treat this as a coach grade, permanent label, or proof of character.
- Recommended display language: Team-first plays: helped teammates make the next play.
- Type: derived metric using direct stats plus subjective tags where noted.


## Competitive Second Effort

- Purpose: Measure effort quality rather than generic hustle volume.
- Formula/scoring logic: Backed Up Shot + Ground Ball after contest + Won Second Effort tags + Recovery Play tags.
- Required inputs: Backed Up Shot, Ground Ball, Hustle Play, tags.
- Sample calculation: 2 GBs + 1 backup + 1 second-effort tag = 4 second-effort plays.
- Minimum sample size: One game; stronger with 3+ games.
- Suitable age groups: All.
- Limitations: Must distinguish purposeful effort from running without tactical value.
- Possible misleading interpretations: Do not treat this as a coach grade, permanent label, or proof of character.
- Recommended display language: Second-effort plays: stayed in the play and created another chance.
- Type: derived metric using direct stats plus subjective tags where noted.


## Direct vs derived vs subjective vs inferred

- Direct statistics: goals, assists, ground balls, clears, saves, turnovers, faceoff wins/losses.
- Derived metrics: possession quality, process impact, response after mistake.
- Subjective tags: supported ball carrier, good decision unsuccessful execution, protected the middle.
- Inferred conclusions: confidence, competitiveness, leadership, and emotional response must be phrased cautiously and never as certainty.
